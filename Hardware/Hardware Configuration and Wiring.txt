## Hardware Configuration and Wiring

This driver uses a 74HC595 Shift Register to connect the ATmega32 to an HD44780 Character LCD in 4-bit mode. This changes the required pins from 7 down to only 3 pins.

### Microcontroller to 74HC595 Connections
These pins are defined in the lcd_serial.h file and can be changed to any other pins. By default, they use PORTC:
* PC5 is connected to SH_CP (Shift Clock)
* PC6 is connected to DS (Serial Data)
* PC7 is connected to ST_CP (Latch Clock)

### 74HC595 to HD44780 LCD Connections
The output pins of the shift register are connected to the LCD control and data lines like this:
* Q0 is connected to RS
* Q1 is connected to E
* Q2 is connected to D7
* Q3 is connected to D6
* Q4 is connected to D5
* Q5 is connected to D4

Note: LCD pins D0, D1, D2, and D3 are not connected because the LCD works in 4-bit mode. The LCD R/W pin must be connected directly to GND.

### 74HC595 Control Pins
To make the shift register work correctly, these two pins must be connected to fixed voltages:
* OE pin must be connected directly to GND
* MR pin must be connected directly to VCC
